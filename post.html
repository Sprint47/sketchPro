What is GOC ? and a brief idea about this concept.

GOC stands for General Organic Chemistry, covers all the fundamental concept that how a chemical reactions will works, what is the mechanism of this reaction, which reactant Intermidiate will take part in this reaction and the reaction occurs determine whether it is Acidic or Basic and which effect will apply to this reaction. In this article we will discuss one by one in briefly.

Before going to the deep, first we need to understand about Reactant Intermidiate. so, basically REACTANT INTERMIDIATE is a chemical substances that will conduct and faster the reaction whenever a certain reagent under goes chemical reaction. As a result the formation of product takes a least of time.

A common example of Reactant Intermidiate is cumene [C9H12] which is made from benzene [C6H6] and propylene [C3H6] and used to make acetone [C3H6O] and phenol [C6H5OH] in the cumene process.

studying GOC helps a lot by solving many chmical reaction, understanding mechanism of reactions and much more.

Now let's talk about CARBOANAION,CARBOANAION & FREE RADICAL.

Whenever a carbon containing compound dissociation occur then carbon donating it's electron to other as a result it becomes electron deficient. This is called carbcataion [C+] , If carbon accepting it's electron from another then this is called carboanaion [C-] & if compound having bond break equally then each element accquire equal amount of electron. This is called Free radical [C.]

Now let's talk about a very important concept of GOC as well as in Organic chemistry, i.e Electrofile and Nucleofile.

The word Electrofile is made by two words "Electro" & "Phile(loving)", i.e electron loving. Electrofile species are electron deficient, they always looking for accepting electron from others. They are either positive charge or neutral with no lone pair of electron. They are represented by the symbol E+. Generally they are Lewis Acid category.

Some of the Examples of electrophiles are:‚Äî hydronium ion (H3O+), boron trifluoride (BF3), aluminum chloride (AlCl3) and the halogen molecules fluorine (F 2), chlorine (Cl2), bromine (Br2), iodine (I2).

Nucleofile species always electron rich compound, or you can say they are always a active donor. They are either negatively charged or having one or more lone pair or of electron / free radicals. Generally ,they are Lewis Base category.

Some examples of Nucleofile are --->
(1) Chlorine (Cl) has 3 lone pairs of electrons in just its atomic form. So it can donate them to other electron-deficient atoms or molecules by getting attached to them.
(2) OH- can act as a great nucleophile because of its electronegativity.
(3) NH3 has a lone pair of electrons. Hence it is a nucleophile.

We covered almost all basic concepts of GOC. Now it's time to discuss what are the major effect connect with it.

On the basis of stability there are mainly two types of effect occurs, such as :- Permanent effect and Temporary effect. Permanent effect showed compound having greater stability as compared to Temporary effect. Permanent effect include:-
(1) Inductive effect,
(2) Hyper Conjugation effect,
(3) Mesomeric Effect
Temporary effect include:-
(a) Electromeric Effect.
